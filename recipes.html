Greek Lentil Stew of Power

The meal that every Greek child hates, because it's "too healthy" and every adult loves, because it's simply delicious. Serve without the feta and you have an amazing vegan dish!


Ingredients

(For 4-6 portions)

  • 250 g lentils
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 1 onion
  • 2 celery sticks
  • 4 cloves of garlic, finely chopped
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 2 teaspoons cumin
  • 1 or 2 teaspoons cayenne pepper
  • 1 tin chopped tomatoes
  • 2 vegetable cubes in 1.5 litres of water
  • salt and pepper

To Serve

  • Bread
  • Feta

Method


  1. Place a large pot over medium heat and add some olive oil.
  2. Finely chop the onions and add them in the pot. Fry them for around 5 minutes.
  3. Coarsely chop the carrots and celery and add to the pot. Fry for another 5 minutes.
  4. Add the bay leaves, cumin, cayenne and garlic and fry for 1 minute.
  5. Stir in the lentils, tomatoes and stock
  6. Cover the pot and allow to simmer on medium heat for 45 minutes.
  7. Check that the lentils are soft, and add salt and pepper to taste.

Pirate Easter Cheese Tarts

Labropites, or Easter Cheese Tarts are a traditional Easter delicacy of Kos. Full of silly amounts of cheese and perfect for accompanying coffee or beer. They also keep well in the freezer.


Ingredients

(For 12-15 tarts)

For the dough:

  • 500gr plain flour
  • 1 + 1/3 cup of yoghurt
  • 2/3 cup melted butter
  • 1 big egg, beaten
  • 2 tsp baking powder
  • 1 tsp salt

For the filling:

  • 250g cheddar grated
  • 500g feta
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 1/2 tsp cinammon
  • 1/4 tsp pepper
  • 1/4 tsp nutmeg

For the glaze:

  • 1 egg yolk
  • 2 tsp milk
  • A little bit of nutmeg

Method


  1. Mix the flour and baking powder in a bowl.
  2. Mix the yogurt, butter and egg with a whisk in another bowl and gradually add the flour mixture.
  3. Make evenly sized balls of the dough and turn each of them into discs, big enough for your muffin tin or rammekins
  4. Oil the muffin tin and place the dough discs in.
  5. Make the filling mixture by mixing the cheeses and squishing the mix with a fork until it becomes crumbly. Add the beaten eggs and spices.
  6. Fill the tarts up to the top.
  7. Brush them with the glaze.
  8. Bake for 30-35mins on 180 degrees.

New Wave of Great Pea Stew (NWGPS)

This pea stew, or "Peas" as it is simply called in Greece, is an easy and delicious one-pot meal. Have it on its own (vegan) or add chicken or white fish.


Ingredients

(For 4 portions)

  • 2-3 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 3-4 medium sized carrots coarsely chopped
  • 1 onion finely chopped
  • 2-3 medium sized potatoes coarsely chopped
  • 3 cloves of garlic
  • 100g passata
  • 150ml red wine
  • 300g water
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 2 star anise
  • 400g chopped tomatoes
  • 1 vegetable stock cube
  • 400g frozen peas

Method


  1. Heat the olive oil in a saucepan and then fry the onion for 5 minutes.
  2. Add the carrot and potatoes and fry for another 5 minutes while keeping the pan covered.
  3. Add the garlic and fry for a minute.
  4. Add the passata and continue cooking for another minute.
  5. Pour in the wine and cook until the alcohol evaporates.
  6. Add the cinnamon, the star anise, the water and chopped tomatoes.
  7. Season, cover and cook for 30 minutes on medium to low heat.
  8. Add the peas in the saucepan and cook for a further 10 minutes.
